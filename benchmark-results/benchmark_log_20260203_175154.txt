============================================================
                    ACADEMIC SOURCES
============================================================

[1] Zhu et al., 'Measuring the Latency and Pervasiveness
    of TLS Certificate Revocation'
    Passive and Active Measurements Conference (PAM), 2016
    https://ant.isi.edu/~johnh/PAPERS/Zhu16a.pdf

    Key findings:
    - Median OCSP latency: 19-20ms (2015-2016 measurements)
    - Previous study (Stark et al., 2012): 291ms median
    - Long tail: <0.1% of requests took 5s to 8 minutes
    - OCSP adds ~10% overhead to TLS handshake time
    - 94% of OCSP traffic served by CDNs
    - With connection reuse: 10ms median
    - Median TLS handshake delay: 242ms

[2] Liu et al., 'An End-to-End Measurement of Certificate
    Revocation in the Web's PKI'
    ACM Internet Measurement Conference (IMC), 2015
    https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2815675.2815685

    Key findings:
    - 8% of certificates served on the Internet are revoked
    - CRL files can be up to 76MB in size
    - OCSP Stapling deployed on only 3% of certificates
    - Mobile browsers NEVER check revocation status
    - Chrome's CRLSet covers only 0.35% of revocations

[3] Netcraft, 'Certificate revocation and the performance
    of OCSP', April 2013
    https://www.netcraft.com/blog/certificate-revocation-and-the-performance-of-ocsp

    Key findings:
    - OCSP responder reliability varies significantly by CA
    - Some CAs had >6% request failure rate
    - EV certificates require multiple OCSP checks (chain)
    - CloudFlare reported 30% improvement with OCSP stapling

============================================================
                 TLS HANDSHAKE TIMES
============================================================

From Zhu et al. (2016) and industry measurements:
    - Median TLS handshake time: 242ms [Zhu 2016]
    - TLS 1.2 full handshake: 2 round-trips (2-RTT)
    - TLS 1.3 full handshake: 1 round-trip (1-RTT)
    - TLS 1.3 resumption: 0 round-trips (0-RTT)
    - Typical range: 50-300ms depending on network latency
